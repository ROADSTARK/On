The Minister of Ministry of Tourism Arts and Culture
Abla Dzifa Gomashie
She was appointed by President John Dramani Mahama in January 2025.
Background on Dzifa Gomashie
Gomashie is an actress, film producer, and politician
She is the Member of Parliament for the Ketu South constituency

She has a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree and an MPhil in African Studies from the University of Ghana
She previously served as the Deputy Minister of Tourism, Culture, and Creative Arts
About the Ministry of Tourism, Arts, and Culture
The Ministry of Tourism, Arts, and Culture (MoTAC) is responsible for promoting and developing tourism in Ghana
The ministry also aims to improve the capital city and create a positive environmental impact.

The Deputy Minister of Tourism Arts and Culture
Mark Okraku-Mantey
Mark Okraku-Mantey is the Deputy Minister of Tourism, Arts, and Culture for Ghana.
He has held this role in the Ministry of Tourism, Arts, and Culture (MoTAC).

Background:
He was previously the acting President of the Creative Arts Council
He was also a Programs Director at the Multimedia group, CEO of Slip Entertainment, and involved in film and drama executive productions
Role: He has participated in delegations to the World Heritage Committee
He has represented the sector minister at events to promote Ghana's heritage
He has called for collaboration and innovation to preserve and promote Ghana's heritage
Decision-making:
He has clarified that the main minister makes decisions, not him
He has urged management to work together to achieve his set targets for the sector
